## Choosing a PVC Profile

**Profile** is the foundation of a window. Its quality and design determine the service life and energy efficiency of the structure.

### Main Profile Parameters

- **Profile width**: from 60 to 80 mm. For typical apartments, the optimal choice is 70 mm
- **Number of chambers**: from 3 to 8. Minimum for an apartment is 5 chambers
- **Outer wall thickness**: at least 2.8 mm (class A per EN 12608)
- **Reinforcement**: steel or combined

> **UWS Expert Tip:** For apartments on middle floors, a 70mm profile with 5-6 chambers is sufficient. This is the optimal price-to-quality ratio. In Kyiv, this is the most popular option for typical panel and brick buildings.

### Comparison of Popular Profile Systems

| Parameter | 60 mm (Economy) | 70 mm (Standard) | 80 mm (Premium) |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Chambers | 3-4 | 5-6 | 6-8 |
| Thermal Insulation | average | good | excellent |
| Sound Insulation | up to 30 dB | up to 38 dB | up to 45 dB |
| Price Category | economy | average | higher |

## Types of Glass Units

**Glass unit** occupies up to 80% of the window area and is responsible for thermal and sound insulation.

### Single-Chamber Glass Unit
- Consists of 2 panes, 1 chamber
- Thermal insulation: R = 0.32 m²·K/W
- Suitable for unheated rooms or as a temporary solution

### Double-Chamber Glass Unit
- Consists of 3 panes, 2 chambers
- Thermal insulation: R = 0.49 m²·K/W
- **Most popular choice for apartments**

### Triple-Chamber Glass Unit
- Consists of 4 panes, 3 chambers
- Thermal insulation: R = 0.63 m²·K/W
- Recommended for top floors and corner apartments

### Additional Glass Unit Options
- **Low-emissivity coating (i-glass)** — reduces heat loss by 30-40%
- **Tinting** — protection from sun rays
- **Noise protection** — asymmetric glass of different thickness
- **Safety glass (triplex)** — breaks into small fragments

> **UWS Expert Tip:** Even the most expensive profile won't deliver desired results without a quality glass unit. We recommend choosing a double-glazed unit with low-emissivity coating — this is the ideal option for most apartments.

## Hardware and Mechanisms

**Hardware** is the "hands" of a window. Its quality determines how comfortably and long you can use the window.

### Main Hardware Elements

1. **Hinges** — hold the sash, ensure rotation
2. **Locks** — fix the sash in the closed position
3. **Handle** — controls the opening mechanism
4. **Comb** — regulates micro-ventilation
5. **Restrictors** — protect against unauthorized opening

### Opening Types

- **Turn** — standard opening inward
- **Tilt** — sash tilts from the top for ventilation
- **Turn-tilt** — most common option, combines both modes
- **Parallel-slide** — for balconies and loggias
- **Lift-slide** — for large openings

### Hardware Quality

Choose hardware from well-known European brands:

- **MACO** (Austria) — reliability and durability
- **ROTO** (Germany) — wide range
- **Siegenia** (Germany) — innovative solutions

> **Important:** Cheap hardware can fail after just 2-3 years. This leads to leaks, drafts, and inability to close the window properly.

## Energy Efficiency Classes

Energy efficiency class determines how well a window retains heat in the room.

### Energy Efficiency Scale

- **Class G** — minimal efficiency, for unheated rooms
- **Class F** — basic level, for temporary residence
- **Class E** — acceptable level for budget solutions
- **Class D** — standard level for most apartments
- **Class C** — increased efficiency
- **Class B** — high efficiency, recommended for apartments
- **Class A** — very high efficiency
- **Class A+** — maximum efficiency
- **Class S** — premium segment, best performance

### What Affects Energy Efficiency Class

1. **Profile** — number of chambers and width
2. **Glass unit** — number of panes and coating
3. **Hardware** — quality of seals
4. **Installation** — correctness of installation

> **UWS Expert Tip:** For a standard apartment, class B is the optimal choice. It provides good thermal insulation without extra costs. If your apartment has large window openings or faces north, choose class A or higher.
