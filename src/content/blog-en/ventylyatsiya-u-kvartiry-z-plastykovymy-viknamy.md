## Why vinyl windows create ventilation problems

Old wooden windows always had micro-gaps through which air constantly circulated. This was natural ventilation. Vinyl windows, on the other hand, provide perfect tightness — and this is their main advantage for insulation, but at the same time a serious problem for indoor climate. In Kyiv apartments, where most buildings have central ventilation, this problem manifests itself especially acutely.

**What happens when windows are airtight:**

- Carbon dioxide accumulates in the room
- Oxygen levels decrease
- A feeling of stuffiness appears
- Sleep quality deteriorates
- Headaches and fatigue may occur
- Mold appears on walls due to excess moisture

> Fact: According to the World Health Organization, indoor air quality can be 2-5 times worse than outdoor air if proper air exchange is not provided.

## Solving the ventilation problem

Fortunately, there are several effective ways to ensure normal air exchange in an apartment with vinyl windows:

### 1. Ventilation valves

This is the most effective solution. There are two main types:

**[Supply valves](/service-details-install)** — installed directly in the window frame and provide fresh air supply. They can be:
- Manual — with adjustable air supply
- Automatic — respond to humidity and temperature levels
- Combined — combine both functions

**Exhaust valves** — installed in the wall or ventilation duct and provide removal of stale air.

### 2. Micro-ventilation

Most modern vinyl windows have a micro-ventilation function. When the handle is turned to a 45° angle, the sash moves slightly away from the frame, creating a gap for air exchange. This allows air to circulate without significant heat loss.

**Advantages of micro-ventilation:**
- No additional equipment needed
- Can be used at any time
- Minimal heat loss
- Easy to use

### 3. Proper airing

Even without additional equipment, you can ensure normal ventilation by following simple rules:

- Air the apartment 2-3 times a day for 10-15 minutes
- Create a cross-draft — open windows on opposite sides of the apartment
- In winter, air more frequently but for shorter periods — 5-10 minutes

> Tip: The most effective airing occurs when windows in the room and kitchen are open — this creates air flow through the apartment.

## How to choose a ventilation valve

When choosing a ventilation valve, pay attention to the following parameters:

| Parameter | Optimal value |
|-----------|---------------|
| Capacity | 20-40 cu.m/hour per person |
| Noise level | No more than 30 dB |
| Heat loss | Minimal |

## Common ventilation mistakes

Many people, trying to solve the problem, make typical mistakes:

**❌ Blocked ventilation ducts**
Check the draft in the ventilation grilles. If a paper sheet doesn't stick to the grille — the duct is blocked and needs cleaning.

**❌ Drying laundry indoors**
A single pair of wet shoes releases up to 300ml of moisture. Laundry on a clothesline in the room is a serious source of humidity.

**❌ Using airtight film on windows**
Some install airtight film for insulation, completely blocking air exchange. This leads to catastrophic deterioration of air quality.

**❌ Permanently closed windows**
Even with ventilation valves, it's recommended to periodically open windows for full airing.

> Important: If you have ventilation valves installed, don't close them. They're designed for constant operation and provide the minimum necessary air exchange even in a closed room.
