## Cold and Warm Glazing: What to Choose

The main difference between glazing types is the thermal insulation level.

### Cold Glazing

Uses lightweight aluminum profile without insulation and single glass or thin glass unit. This glazing protects from wind, rain, and dust, but doesn't provide thermal insulation.

**When to choose cold glazing:**
- Balcony or loggia is not heated
- Only protection from weather is needed
- Limited budget
- Balcony is used as storage

**Advantages of cold glazing:**
- Low price
- Lightweight construction (doesn't overload balcony slab)
- Quick installation

**Disadvantages:**
- In winter, temperature doesn't differ from outside
- Condensation may form

### Warm Glazing

Uses plastic or aluminum-polymer profile with insulation and double-glazed unit. Provides high thermal insulation and allows using the balcony as a living space.

**When to choose warm glazing:**
- Balcony is heated or heating is planned
- Balcony is used as an office or relaxation room
- High thermal insulation is needed

**Advantages:**
- High thermal insulation
- Reduced street noise
- No condensation

**Disadvantages:**
- High price
- Heavy weight (slab strength needs to be checked)
- Requires wall and floor insulation

> **Expert Tip:** Before choosing glazing type, be sure to check the balcony slab condition. Warm glazing is significantly heavier, and old balconies may not withstand the additional load.

## Types of Balcony Profiles

The profile is the foundation of the window frame, which determines thermal insulation, strength, and service life of the glazing.

### Aluminum Profile

Lightweight, strong, and corrosion-resistant. Used for cold glazing.

**Advantages:** lightweight, high strength, corrosion resistance, durability (over 50 years).

**Disadvantages:** poor thermal insulation, high price for insulated options.

### Plastic Profile

The most common option for warm glazing. Provides good thermal insulation and affordable price.

**Advantages:** high thermal insulation, affordable price, wide color selection.

**Disadvantages:** heavier than aluminum, may yellow from sun.

### Aluminum-Polymer Profile

Combines aluminum strength and polymer thermal insulation. Perfect for warm glazing with high requirements.

**Advantages:** high strength, excellent thermal insulation, weather resistance.

**Disadvantages:** high price.

## Glass Units for Balcony Glazing

The glass unit is the most important glazing element that determines thermal and sound insulation.

### Single-Chamber Glass Unit

Consists of two panes and one air chamber. Suitable for cold glazing or unheated rooms.

### Double-Chamber Glass Unit

Consists of three panes and two air chambers. Provides better thermal and sound insulation. Perfect for warm glazing.

### Special Glass Units

- **Energy-saving.** Have coating that reflects heat into the room.
- **Tinted.** Protect from excessive sunlight.
- **Multi-functional.** Combine energy-saving and tinting.

> **Expert Tip:** For warm balcony glazing, we recommend a double-glazed unit with energy-saving coating. This will provide maximum thermal insulation and reduce heating costs.

## Installation and Insulation Stages

Installing windows on a balcony is a complex process that requires experience and knowledge.

### 1. Preparatory Stage

- Balcony slab condition assessment
- Glazing type and profile selection
- Measurements and design
- Documentation approval

### 2. Old Glazing Removal

- Removing old frames
- Preparing the open structure
- Surface cleaning

### 3. New Glazing Installation

- Frame installation by level
- Fixing with brackets
- Glass unit installation
- Seam sealing

### 4. Insulation (for warm glazing)

- Wall insulation with polystyrene or mineral wool
- Floor insulation
- Ceiling insulation
- Slope finishing

### 5. Final Finishing

- Windowsill installation
- Wall cladding
- Sill installation
- Painting or facing

### Common Mistakes

- **Incorrect slab strength assessment.** Leads to deformation or collapse.
- **No insulation.** Without it, warm glazing doesn't perform its function.
- **Poor sealing.** Leads to leaks and heat loss.
- **Incorrect profile choice.** Mismatch with operating conditions.
