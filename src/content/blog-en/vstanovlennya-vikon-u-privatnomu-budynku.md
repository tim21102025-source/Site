Window installation in private houses has a number of features that differ from apartment installation. Wall thickness, ventilation, large window openings — all this requires professional approach. In Kyiv and suburbs, private houses are becoming increasingly popular, and proper window installation is the key to comfort and energy efficiency.

## House Opening Features

Window openings in private houses are significantly different from apartment ones.

### Wall Thickness

- **Apartment:** 20-40 cm (typical panel buildings)
- **Private house:** 40-80 cm (brick, aerated concrete, wood)

This means:
- Windowsills will be deeper (from 30 to 60 cm)
- More mounting foam is needed to fill gaps
- Frame insertion depth must be considered

### Opening Sizes

Private houses often have:
- **Large window openings** — from 1.5 to 3 m wide
- **Floor-to-ceiling windows** — especially in living rooms
- **Non-standard shapes** — arched, triangular, panoramic

### Wall Material

| Material | Installation Features |
|----------|----------------------|
| Brick | Strong, but requires deep fastening |
| Aerated concrete | Light, but brittle — special fasteners |
| Wood | Mobile — settlement compensators |
| Frame | Light, but requires additional insulation |

> **UWS Expert Tip:** Before installing windows, be sure to measure the opening depth. This determines the windowsill type and frame fastening method.

## Choosing Windows for Different Rooms

Each room in a private house has its own window requirements.

### Living Room

- **Size:** large windows, preferably floor-to-ceiling
- **Configuration:** panoramic glazing or window-doors to terrace
- **Hardware:** turn-tilt or parallel-slide
- **Features:** good view, lots of light

### Bedroom

- **Size:** medium windows (1.2-1.8 m wide)
- **Configuration:** standard rectangular
- **Hardware:** with micro-ventilation and blocking
- **Features:** good sound insulation, possibility of complete darkening

### Children's Room

- **Size:** medium windows with opening restrictions
- **Configuration:** rectangular with restrictors
- **Hardware:** with blocking and opening restrictors
- **Features:** safety glass (triplex), opening restrictions

### Kitchen

- **Size:** medium windows (1-1.5 m wide)
- **Configuration:** with ventilation gap
- **Hardware:** with micro-ventilation
- **Features:** protection from grease and steam

### Bathroom

- **Size:** small windows (0.6-1 m)
- **Configuration:** frosted glass or tinted glass unit
- **Hardware:** with micro-ventilation
- **Features:** increased humidity — waterproofing is necessary

> **UWS Expert Tip:** Think through window configuration for each room in advance. This will allow selecting optimal sizes and hardware types. In private houses in Kyiv, especially in suburbs, we recommend using windows with increased thermal insulation.

## Installation and Insulation

Window installation in private houses has its own technological features.

### Opening Preparation

1. **Geometry check** — walls must be vertical and horizontal
2. **Cleaning** — remove dirt, dust, and old material residues
3. **Waterproofing** — treat walls with waterproofing compound

### Frame Fastening

- **For brick:** anchors at least 10 cm deep
- **For aerated concrete:** special dowels or adhesive fastening
- **For wood:** self-tapping screws considering settlement (1-2 cm gap)

### Installation Seam Insulation

Outer layer:
1. **Vapor-permeable tape** — releases moisture outward
2. **Frost-resistant mounting foam** — for filling gaps
3. **Anti-adhesion tape** — prevents foam from sticking to wall

Inner layer:
1. **Vapor-impermeable tape** — does not allow moisture into the seam
2. **Sealant** — for final sealing

### Windowsill and Sill Installation

- **Windowsill:** deep (from 40 cm), strong, with drip edge
- **Sill:** aluminum or steel, with slope from wall

> **Important:** Don't use regular mounting foam for the outer seam. It's not frost-resistant and moisture-resistant. Always use frost-resistant materials.

## Ventilation and Climate Control

In private houses, window ventilation plays a special role.

### Ventilation Types

1. **Micro-ventilation** — sash opening angle 2-5 mm
2. **Gap ventilation** — gap 5-15 mm wide
3. **Supply valves** — ensure constant fresh air flow
4. **Exhaust grilles** — for removing used air

### Ventilation Recommendations

- **For each room** — equip a ventilation valve
- **For kitchen** — install exhaust hood above the stove
- **For bathroom** — moisture removal and ventilation are mandatory
- **For bedroom** — micro-ventilation for comfortable sleep

### Humidity Control

- **Optimal humidity:** 40-60%
- **Above 70%** — mold risk
- **Below 30%** — dry air, discomfort

> **UWS Expert Tip:** For private houses, we recommend a ventilation system with supply valves on each window. This ensures constant fresh air flow without drafts.

## Care and Maintenance

Proper window care in private houses will extend their service life.

### Daily Care

- **Ventilation:** 2-3 times a day for 10-15 minutes
- **Glass wiping:** with damp cloth without detergents
- **Seal check:** for damage and cracks

### Annual Care

- **Hardware lubrication:** with silicone lubricant
- **Sash adjustment:** checking fit tightness
- **Drain cleaning:** removing dirt and leaves

### Step-by-Step Care

| Period | What to Do |
|--------|------------|
| Weekly | Wipe frames and glass |
| Monthly | Check hardware |
| Seasonally | Lubricate and adjust |
| Annually | Full inspection and maintenance |

### When to Contact Professionals

- Drafts or leaks
- Inability to close sash properly
- Condensation between glass panes
- Seal damage
