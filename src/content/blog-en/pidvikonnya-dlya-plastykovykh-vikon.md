A windowsill is not just a decorative window element. It performs important functions: protects the wall from moisture, creates an additional work surface, and completes the overall look of the window opening. In this article, we'll examine how to properly choose a windowsill for plastic windows, what materials exist, and what to look for when buying. Specifically, we'll examine issues relevant to Kyiv residents, where windowsill installation is a sought-after service.

## Why You Need a Windowsill

A windowsill performs several key functions:

- **Wall protection.** Prevents moisture and condensation from penetrating the wall structure, which is especially important for apartments with increased humidity.
- **Aesthetic function.** Completes the overall look of the window, hiding the frame-wall junction.
- **Additional surface.** Creates space for flowers, decor, or a work area.
- **Thermal insulation.** Helps reduce heat loss through the window opening.

> **Expert Tip:** If the windowsill is installed incorrectly, gaps may form through which warm air will escape outside. Therefore, it's worth trusting installation to professionals.

## Types of Windowsills by Material

The market offers several types of windowsills, each with its own advantages and disadvantages.

### PVC (Plastic) Windowsills

The most popular option due to affordable price and simple installation. PVC windowsills are lightweight, moisture-resistant, and don't require special care. They perfectly match plastic windows in color and texture.

**Advantages:**
- Low price
- Lightweight
- Simple installation
- Moisture resistance

**Disadvantages:**
- May yellow from direct sunlight
- Less resistant to mechanical damage
- Limited color selection

### Stone Windowsills

Made from natural or stone. Differ in high strength, durability, and premium appearance. Perfectly suited for kitchens and bathrooms.

**Advantages:**
- High strength and wear resistance
- Moisture and temperature resistance
- Luxurious appearance
- Durability (over 50 years)

**Disadvantages:**
- High price
- Heavy weight
- Complex installation

### Wooden Windowsills

A classic option that looks great in country or eco-style interiors. Wood is natural, pleasant to touch, and creates a cozy atmosphere.

**Advantages:**
- Natural material
- Pleasant texture
- Eco-friendliness

**Disadvantages:**
- Require regular treatment
- Moisture-resistant only with proper treatment
- May deform from temperature changes

### Acrylic Windowsills

A modern material that combines the advantages of PVC and stone. Acrylic windowsills have a smooth, non-porous surface that's easy to clean and doesn't absorb dirt.

**Advantages:**
- Lightweight and strength
- Stain resistance
- Color variety
- Scratch repair possibility

## How to Choose Windowsill Size

The right windowsill size is the key to quality installation and aesthetic window appearance.

### Width

Standard windowsill width for living rooms is 20-25 cm. For kitchens and bathrooms, you can choose narrower options (15-20 cm), as there's usually less free space there.

**Important:** Windowsill width shouldn't cover the heating radiator by more than 1/3, otherwise heat won't circulate normally around the room.

### Length

Windowsill length is determined by the window opening width. The windowsill should be 3-5 cm wider than the opening on each side to fit tightly against the wall and cover the junction.

### Thickness

Standard windowsill thickness is 20-40 mm. Thickness depends on material and load. For stone windowsills, a thickness of at least 30 mm is recommended.

## Installation and Care Tips

### Installation

Proper windowsill installation includes several stages:

1. **Surface preparation.** Cleaning and leveling the installation location.
2. **Blank cutting.** The windowsill is cut considering slopes and angles.
3. **Installation on substrates.** The windowsill is fixed using special substrates or brackets.
4. **Foaming.** Cavities under the windowsill are filled with mounting foam for thermal and sound insulation.
5. **Final finishing.** Sealing seams and installing decorative caps.

### Care

- For PVC and acrylic windowsills, use mild detergents without abrasive particles.
- Stone windowsills can be washed with regular detergents, but avoid acidic compounds.
- Wooden windowsills require regular application of protective coating (linseed oil or varnish).
- Avoid placing hot objects without stands, especially on PVC surfaces.

### Common Mistakes

- **Incorrect measurement.** Leads to gaps and need for additional processing.
- **No insulation.** Cavity under windowsill without foam becomes a cold bridge.
- **Radiator blocking.** Hinders normal warm air circulation.
- **Using low-quality materials.** Leads to deformation and loss of appearance.
