## Which products are suitable for vinyl windows

Vinyl windows require a special approach to cleaning. The wrong products can damage the frame finish and strip it of its glossy shine. In Kyiv, where dust and exhaust fumes settle on windows, proper care is especially important for maintaining their appearance.

### Best cleaning products:

**1. Soapy solution**
The simplest and safest option. Add 1-2 drops of mild dish soap to a liter of warm water. This is sufficient for regular cleaning.

**2. Specialized PVC cleaners**
You can find products specifically designed for vinyl surfaces in stores. They don't contain abrasives and gently clean dirt.

**3. Vinegar solution**
For removing stains and buildup, use a solution of 1 part vinegar to 4 parts water. This is effective and safe.

**4. Citric acid**
A teaspoon of citric acid per liter of water — an excellent remedy for removing yellowing and stubborn stains.

> Tip: Always test a new product on an inconspicuous area of the frame before full-scale use.

## What you should never do

Some products and methods can permanently damage vinyl windows:

**❌ Abrasive powders and pastes**
Granules leave scratches on the glossy vinyl surface. Even "gentle" powders can be harmful with regular use.

**❌ Rough cloths**
Metal scouring pads, stiff brushes, and even some sponges can leave micro-scratches. Use only soft materials.

**❌ Gasoline, acetone, and solvents**
These substances destroy the vinyl surface and can cause discoloration. Never use them for window cleaning.

**❌ High-pressure water**
A high-pressure water stream can damage seals and penetrate inside the frame. Use only gentle rinsing or a cloth.

**❌ Dry wiping**
Wiping with a dry cloth causes scratches and spreads dust. Always moisten the surface before cleaning.

> Important: If you're unsure about a product's safety — don't risk it. Use a proven soapy solution instead.

## Glass cleaning technique

Glass is the most finicky part of a window. Proper cleaning technique guarantees perfect clarity without streaks.

### Step-by-step instructions:

**Step 1: Preparation**
Dust the frame and glass with a dry brush or vacuum with a soft attachment. This prevents scratches during washing.

**Step 2: Apply solution**
Dampen a soft cloth or microfiber in the soapy solution and apply to the glass. Don't oversaturate the frame — excess water can get under the seal.

**Step 3: Wiping**
Wipe the glass horizontally, moving from top to bottom. Use a rubber squeegee or rubber spatula to remove water.

**Step 4: Polishing**
Use a dry microfiber cloth for final polishing. Use circular motions to achieve perfect clarity.

**Step 5: Inspection**
Look at the glass at an angle to the light. If streaks remain, wipe them separately.

> Tip: The best time to clean windows is on a cloudy day or in the evening. In direct sunlight, the solution dries too quickly, leaving streaks.

## Frame and hardware care

The frame and hardware need separate attention:

### Frame:
- Wipe the frame with a damp cloth after each glass cleaning
- Use a citric acid solution to remove yellowing
- Don't forget hard-to-reach areas — corners and bends

### Hardware:
- Wipe handles and hinges with a damp cloth
- Periodically lubricate moving parts with a graphite pencil
- Don't use harsh chemicals for metal parts

### [Seals](/service-details-repair):
- Wipe rubber seals with a damp cloth
- Periodically treat them with silicone lubricant to maintain elasticity
- Don't allow dirt to accumulate in seal folds
