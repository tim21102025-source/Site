## Introduction

Condensation on windows is a common problem faced by many plastic window owners, especially in Kyiv during winter. Water droplets on the glass, wet window slopes, and mold growth — these are not just aesthetic issues but also threats to health and structural integrity.

In this article, we'll examine the causes of condensation and effective methods to prevent it.

## Why Does Condensation Appear?

Condensation occurs due to a physical process: moist air, when in contact with a cold surface, cools and loses its ability to retain moisture. As a result, water is released in the form of droplets.

### Main Causes:

**1. High Indoor Humidity**

This is the primary cause. Normal indoor humidity is 40-60%. Above 60%, the risk of condensation increases significantly.

**Sources of excess moisture:**
- Cooking without an exhaust hood
- Drying laundry indoors
- Large number of houseplants
- Aquariums
- Respiratory illnesses of residents
- Insufficient ventilation

**2. Temperature Difference**

The greater the temperature difference between outside and inside, the higher the condensation risk. This is especially true in winter when temperatures drop below -10°C.

**3. Thermal Bridges**

Thermal bridges are areas of the structure where heat actively escapes. Most commonly:
- The junction between the glass unit and frame
- Metal structural elements
- Areas where the frame contacts the wall

**4. Improper Installation**

If a window is installed with technology violations (insufficient insulation, no waterproofing), it can lead to condensation.

**5. Blocked Ventilation**

Plastic windows are airtight, and if the building's ventilation system doesn't work properly, moisture isn't expelled outside.

## Methods to Prevent Condensation

### 1. Regular Ventilation

The simplest and most effective method is regular room ventilation.

**Recommended ventilation schedule:**
- **Frequency:** 3-4 times per day
- **Duration:** 10-15 minutes
- **Method:** casement or tilt-and-turn opening

> **Tip:** Ventilation is more effective when creating a cross draft (opening casements on opposite sides of the room). This ensures quick air exchange.

**Types of ventilation:**

- **Short (intensive):** 5-10 minutes with fully open casements. Effectively removes excess moisture.
- **Extended (gentle):** 1-2 hours with slightly open casements. Suitable for continuous ventilation.
- **Night:** leave a casement slightly open overnight (if there are no drafts or cold).

### 2. Humidity Control

**Measuring humidity**

Buy a hygrometer (humidity meter). This is an inexpensive device (from 200 UAH) that helps monitor humidity levels.

**Optimal readings:**
- **Bedroom:** 40-50%
- **Living room:** 40-60%
- **Kitchen:** 40-60% (can rise to 70% during cooking)
- **Bathroom:** 50-70%

**Ways to reduce humidity:**

- **Kitchen exhaust hood:** use the hood every time you cook.
- **Bathroom exhaust:** turn on before showering and leave running for 15-20 minutes after.
- **Don't dry laundry in the room:** use the balcony, bathroom, or a dedicated dryer.
- **Limit the number of plants:** a large number of houseplants increases humidity.
- **Cover pot lids:** use lids during cooking to reduce moisture in the air.

### 3. Room Heating

Even heating helps maintain glass temperature above the dew point.

**Tips:**
- **Don't block radiators:** don't place furniture or curtains in front of radiators. Heat should circulate freely.
- **Use thermal curtains:** if the window is far from the radiator, install a convector or thermal curtain.
- **Maintain temperature:** minimum room temperature is 18-20°C. Lower temperatures increase condensation risk.

### 4. Using Micro-Ventilation

Most modern plastic windows have a micro-ventilation (micro-airing) function. This is a small gap that ensures constant air circulation.

**How to use:**
- Set the handle to the micro-ventilation position (usually a 45° turn)
- Leave the window in this position overnight
- Ensure access to outside air

> **Important:** Micro-ventilation doesn't replace full ventilation but helps maintain normal humidity throughout the day.

### 5. Ventilation Valves

If regular ventilation isn't possible or convenient, install ventilation valves.

**Types of valves:**
- **Wall-mounted:** installed in the wall, providing constant fresh air supply.
- **Window-mounted:** installed directly in the window, no renovation needed.
- **Supply valves:** provide outside air with adjustable flow.

**Benefits of ventilation valves:**
- Constant air circulation without drafts
- Automatic air supply adjustment
- Heat retention (valves have filters and controls)
- Reduced outside noise

## Additional Measures

### 1. Slope Insulation

Uninsulated slopes are the main thermal bridge. If slopes aren't insulated, cold penetrates through the wall and cools the window frame.

**Insulation options:**
- Mineral wool + plaster
- Foam board + plaster
- Thermal plaster
- PVC panels with insulation

### 2. Glass Unit Replacement

If your windows have a single-chamber glass unit without energy-saving coating, this could be the cause of condensation. Replacing it with a double-chamber unit with a low-emissivity coating significantly reduces the risk. UWS performs [glass unit replacement](/service-details-glass) in Kyiv with a warranty on all work.

### 3. Hardware Adjustment

Check whether the sash presses tightly against the frame. Insufficient pressure allows cold air inside and cools the glass.

### 4. Using Films

Special energy-saving films can be applied to the glass unit. They reflect heat back into the room and raise the glass temperature.

## What Doesn't Help with Condensation?

Some "folk" methods are ineffective or even harmful:

- **Salt or chalk:** don't help, just create a mess.
- **Newspapers on the windowsill:** don't solve the problem, just mask the consequences.
- **Opening windows overnight without control:** can lead to overcooling and drafts.
- **Using heaters near windows:** effective but energy-intensive and potentially dangerous.

## When Is Window Replacement Needed?

In some cases, condensation indicates serious problems that can't be solved by preventive measures:

- **Single-chamber glass unit without energy-saving** — replace with a double-chamber energy-saving unit.
- **Class B profile (wall thickness 2.5 mm)** — replace with Class A profile (3 mm).
- **Improper installation** — reinstallation with slope insulation.
- **Very old windows** (over 15-20 years) — replace with modern energy-efficient windows.

## Conclusion

Condensation on windows isn't a death sentence. In most cases, the problem can be solved without window replacement through:

- Regular ventilation (3-4 times per day for 10-15 minutes)
- Humidity control (40-60%)
- Proper heating (don't block radiators)
- Using micro-ventilation or ventilation valves
- Slope insulation
