## Key Material Characteristics

When choosing windows for a home or apartment in Kyiv, a common question arises: **plastic or aluminum windows** — which better suits your needs? Both materials have unique characteristics worth considering.

**Plastic windows (PVC)** consist of a polyvinyl chloride profile with a chamber construction featuring air cavities. These chambers provide excellent thermal and sound insulation. PVC profiles are lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and don't require painting.

**Aluminum windows** are made from aluminum alloy with varying wall thicknesses. Modern aluminum profiles are equipped with thermal breaks that significantly improve their thermal insulation properties.

> **Important:** The material choice depends on the building type, climate conditions, and your priorities regarding aesthetics and functionality.

## Thermal Insulation Comparison

Thermal insulation is one of the most important parameters when choosing windows, especially in Kyiv's climate with cold winters and hot summers.

**Plastic windows** have a thermal conductivity coefficient of **0.14-0.19 W/(m·K)**, providing excellent thermal insulation. The chamber construction of the profile creates an additional air barrier.

**Aluminum windows** without thermal breaks have a coefficient of **2.0-3.5 W/(m·K)**, which is significantly worse. However, modern models with thermal breaks improve performance to **0.8-1.2 W/(m·K)**.

| Parameter | Plastic Windows | Aluminum Windows (with thermal break) |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------------------------------|
| Thermal conductivity | 0.14-0.19 W/(m·K) | 0.8-1.2 W/(m·K) |
| Thermal protection | Excellent | Good |
| Energy efficiency | High | Medium |

## Lifespan and Maintenance

Window lifespan is an investment in your comfort for years to come.

**Plastic windows:**
- Lifespan: **20-40 years**
- Require regular hardware lubrication (1-2 times per year)
- Don't require painting
- Easy to clean from dirt
- May yellow under direct sunlight

**Aluminum windows:**
- Lifespan: **30-50 years**
- Practically maintenance-free
- Resistant to UV and corrosion
- May have scratches that are easily polished out
- Available in a wide range of colors

> **Tip:** If you're looking for "lifetime" windows, properly installed aluminum products will last longer. But plastic windows are a more practical choice for most apartments.

## Price Comparison

Window cost is an important factor for most buyers. Let's compare market prices.

**Plastic windows:**
- From **3,500 to 8,000 UAH** for a standard window (1.5×1.5 m)
- Depends on profile (5-7 chambers), hardware, and glass unit
- Budget option with good quality

**Aluminum windows:**
- From **6,000 to 15,000 UAH** for a similar window
- Higher price due to expensive material and manufacturing
- More expensive but more durable

**Factors affecting price:**
- Profile type and chambers
- Hardware quality (Roto, MACO, Siegenia)
- [Glass unit](/service-details-glass) type (standard, energy-saving, noise-insulating)
- Additional options ([mosquito nets](/service-details-moskitki), flashing, windowsills)

## Where Each Type Works Best

The material choice depends on specific operating conditions.

**Plastic windows are recommended for:**
- Residential apartments and houses
- Office spaces with central heating
- Buildings with high thermal insulation requirements
- Budget projects with good quality

**Aluminum windows are better suited for:**
- Large panoramic structures
- Industrial and commercial spaces
- Buildings with architectural features
- Spaces with high humidity (pools, saunas)
- Facades requiring special design

> **Recommendation:** For a typical Ukrainian apartment or house, plastic windows offer the optimal price-quality-thermal insulation ratio. Aluminum windows are worth considering for custom solutions or commercial properties.
