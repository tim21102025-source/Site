## Myths About Winter Installation

There are several common misconceptions about installing windows in cold weather.

### Myth 1: "Plastic Cracks in Cold Weather"

**This is not true.** Modern PVC profiles withstand temperatures down to -60°C. Plastic does not lose its properties at low temperatures. The only limitation is to avoid mechanical impacts on very cold plastic, as it becomes more brittle.

### Myth 2: "Mounting Foam Doesn't Work in Winter"

**Partially true.** Regular mounting foam may indeed not polymerize at temperatures below 0°C. But there are **frost-resistant mounting foams** that work at temperatures from -10°C to +40°C.

### Myth 3: "Windows Must Be Installed in Summer"

**This is a myth.** Window installation is not facade work that depends on air temperature. With proper technology and appropriate materials, installation can be done at any time of year.

### Myth 4: "You Can't Check Installation Quality in Winter"

**Not true.** On the contrary, it's easier to detect problems in winter — drafts and cold bridges are immediately noticeable. This allows timely correction of deficiencies.

> **Fact:** Most European companies work year-round. In winter, they even have an advantage — fewer queues and faster work completion. In Kyiv, winter installation is also becoming increasingly popular, especially in new buildings.

## Winter Installation Technology

Winter installation differs from summer installation in several important aspects.

### Opening Preparation

1. **Cleaning from snow and ice** — the opening must be dry and clean
2. **Warming the walls** — use a heat gun if necessary
3. **Removing moisture** — moisture can freeze and damage the installation seam

### Frame Installation

- Install the frame only in a dry opening
- Always leave a technological gap between the frame and the wall
- Check the frame level after each fastening stage

### Filling Gaps

- Use **frost-resistant mounting foam**
- Apply foam in thin layers (each no more than 2-3 cm)
- Ensure air access for polymerization

### Insulating the Installation Seam

- Outer layer — vapor-permeable tape
- Inner layer — vapor-impermeable tape
- This prevents condensation in the insulation

## Materials for Winter Installation

Winter installation requires special materials adapted to low temperatures.

### Mounting Foam

| Parameter | Regular Foam | Frost-Resistant Foam |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| Temperature Range | +5°C to +35°C | -10°C to +40°C |
| Polymerization | fast | slow |
| Cost | lower | higher |
| Application | summer | year-round |

### Insulation

- **Mineral wool** — not afraid of frost, but requires waterproofing
- **Polystyrene** — frost-resistant, but brittle at low temperatures
- **Foam tape (PSUL)** — ready-made solution for insulating installation seams

### Waterproofing Tapes

- **Vapor-permeable (outer)** — releases moisture outward
- **Vapor-impermeable (inner)** — does not allow moisture into the seam

### Sealants

- **Silicone sealants** — work at temperatures down to -40°C
- **Acrylic sealants** — for indoor work, not frost-resistant

> **Important:** Don't save on materials for winter installation. Cheap mounting foam or lack of vapor barrier can lead to freezing of the installation seam and mold growth.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages of Winter Installation

- **Lower prices** — seasonal discounts from manufacturers and installation companies
- **Faster completion** — fewer queues, work is done faster
- **Easier to detect problems** — drafts and cold bridges are immediately noticeable
- **Comfort in the apartment** — no need to live with open openings in the cold

### Disadvantages of Winter Installation

- **Higher material costs** — frost-resistant foam is more expensive
- **More difficult weather conditions** — snow, wind, short daylight hours
- **Temperature restrictions** — in severe frost (-20°C and below), it's better to postpone installation

### When to Postpone Installation

- Air temperature below -15°C (for standard materials)
- Strong wind (over 10 m/s)
- Precipitation in the form of rain or wet snow
- High humidity (over 80%)

## Tips for Winter Installation

If you decide to install windows in winter, follow these recommendations.

### Preparation

1. **Check the weather** — choose a dry day without precipitation
2. **Prepare the room** — cover furniture with plastic to protect from dust
3. **Ensure heating** — room temperature should be at least +15°C

### During Installation

- Don't rush — frost-resistant foam polymerizes more slowly
- Carefully check the frame fit tightness
- Use a heat gun to warm the opening if necessary

### After Installation

- Don't open windows for 24 hours (for complete foam polymerization)
- Check airtightness after the first night
- Fix all hardware adjustments
